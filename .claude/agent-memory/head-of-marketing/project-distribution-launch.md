**Sales surface:** Own landing page + Stripe checkout (universal in the $299-999 band). NOT Gumroad / Lemon Squeezy for the core product. Lemon Squeezy worth evaluating at launch for tax/VAT handling. USD-only for v1, add EUR at launch if buyers ask.

**Launch channels (sequenced, not parallel):**

| Channel | When | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-launch waitlist + landing page** | M5-M6 of build, 4-6 weeks pre-launch | Email capture, day-1 buyers, design-partner recruitment |
| **Product Hunt** | 4-6 weeks after v1 ships | Every competitor has launched there. The default. |
| **Indie Hackers** | At launch, ongoing | Community post + thread. Long-tail traffic. |
| **Hacker News (Show HN)** | 1-2 weeks after Product Hunt | Risky, high-reward. Sequence matters for SEO boost. |
| **X / Twitter** | Long game, NOT launch tactic | Don't try to copy ShipFast's Marc Lou 135k brand moat — it's a one-person moat, not replicable. |
| **SEO + content** | Continuous, 6+ month play | Long-tail keywords (see [[project-seo-strategy]]). |

**The "after you launch" narrative is a wedge we can claim** — supastarter's own founder publicly markets against it: "Most SaaS boilerplates help you launch fast. But what happens after you launch?" (Jonathan Wilke, X). Use it explicitly in copy and content.

**Beta program (recommended, 10-20 founders):** Test v1 for free in exchange for testimonials. De-risks the wizard, seeds the launch with social proof, and is the source of the first real testimonials (no fake testimonials until post-launch).

**Funnel targets:**
- Visitor → lead: 5-15% (typical for dev tools with strong lead magnet)
- Lead → customer: 1-5% (typical for $299+ products)
- Effective: 0.05%-0.75% (visitor → customer)

**The 195-integration-tests number is a buyer-named category** — buyers explicitly call out the "untested boilerplate" trap. Surface the test count in marketing copy once CI is green.

**How to apply:**
- Don't recommend paid acquisition as the primary channel — economics don't work for a one-time product at our price points.
- Don't recommend copying ShipFast's distribution — it's a personal brand moat.
- For X/Twitter, the play is design-principle content + build-in-public, not launch hype.
- See [[project-seo-strategy]] for the SEO channel deep-dive, [[project-funnel-math]] for the conversion math.
